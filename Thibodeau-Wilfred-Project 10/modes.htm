The modes of a major scale are simply the same major scale played starting from a specific degree. For example, play a major scale from root to root, you get Ionian. Play that same major scale from the 2nd degree to 2nd degree, you get Dorian.

  • C major: C D E F G A B
  • The modes:
    • Ionian: C D E F G A B
    • Dorian: D E F G A B C
    • Phrygian: E F G A B C D
    • Lydian: F G A B C D E
    • Mixolydian: G A B C D E F
    • Aeolian: A B C D E F G
    • Locrian: B C D E F G A

Each mode has it's own sound/feel. Dorian is used a lot in rock and blues. Mixolydian is great for country and bluegrass. Lydian is very jazzy, and used by rock guitarists such as Steve Vai and Joe Satriani.

The melodic minor scale and its modes are often used in jazz and fusion and create more complex and intersting sounds than the modes of the major scale.

The Modes

Melodic Minor (Jazz Minor)
Usage: Use on min/maj chords
Formula: 1 2 b3 4 5 6 7

Mode: Dorian b2
Usage: Use on b9sus4 chords
Formula: 1 b2 b3 4 5 6 b7

Mode: Lydian #5 (Lydian Augmented)
Usage: Use on maj7#5 chords
Formula: 1 2 3 #4 #5 6 7

Mode: Lydian Dominant (Lydian b7)
Usage: Use on 7#11 chords
Formula: 1 2 3 #4 5 6 b7

Mode: Mixolydian b6 (Hindu scale)
Usage: Use on dom7b13 chords
Formula: 1 2 3 4 5 b6 b7

Mode: Aeolian b5
Usage: Use on m7b5 chords
Formula: 1 2 b3 4 b5 b6 b7

Mode: Altered Scale (Super Locrian)
Usage: Use on altered dom chords
Formula: 1 b2 b3 b4 b5 b6 b7
